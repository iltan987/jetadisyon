# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** iltan
**Date:** 2026-03-07

## Session Overview

**Topic:** B2B SaaS order management & AI-powered business intelligence platform for Turkish food businesses operating on major online food ordering marketplaces (Trendyol Go, Yemeksepeti)

**Goals:** Define core feature set and differentiation, AI feature scope, packaging/pricing model, and organic growth strategy — all grounded in real small business needs

### Session Setup

**Core Product Vision:**
- Auto-accept incoming orders from integrated platforms
- Print order receipts (small paper with order info, QR, etc.)
- AI-powered business analytics and actionable suggestions

**Key Differentiators Identified:**
- Simpler, focused feature set (not bloated) — businesses pay only for what they need
- Tiered packaging: basic (auto-accept + print) → analytics → AI insights
- Cheaper than competition
- AI-driven suggestions based on area pricing, sales patterns, weather, special days (bayram etc.)

**AI Feature Scope (Initial Thinking):**
- Area-based price benchmarking (expensive/normal/cheap per product or overall)
- Sales pattern analysis: peak times, top products, day-of-week trends
- External factor correlation: special days (bayram), weather (rain/snow/sun)
- Actionable suggestions: "Discount product X on sunny days to increase sales ~20%"

**Growth Strategy:**
- Start with businesses the founder personally uses and knows (local, family connections)
- Grow through word-of-mouth and recommendations
- Eventually build a marketing website as the product matures
- Trial-first model: 3-6 month free trial → paid subscription

**Technical Context:**
- Solo developer, no time pressure
- Multi-tenant architecture with license management needed
- Integration via API/webhook/websocket (developer portal access pending)
- Stack: Next.js + NestJS + Supabase monorepo, BMAD-driven development
